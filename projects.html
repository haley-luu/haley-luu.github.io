Projects

These projects reflect different parts of my training in epidemiology and public health, including statistical analysis, study planning, field data collection, health disparities research, and scientific communication.

Factors Associated With Infant Birthweight

For my Intermediate Biostatistics final project, I used multivariable linear regression to identify factors associated with infant birthweight, with particular attention to factors associated with lower birthweight.

I completed the analysis in Stata, evaluated model assumptions and diagnostics, interpreted adjusted associations, and presented the analysis in a report organized into Introduction, Methods, Results, and Discussion sections.

D-Penicillamine Treatment and Primary Biliary Cirrhosis

For my Introduction to Biostatistics final project, I analyzed a simulated dataset based on a randomized clinical trial comparing D-penicillamine treatment with placebo among patients with primary biliary cirrhosis.

I used Stata to summarize participant characteristics, compare clinical and biochemical measures, examine differences across histologic disease stages, and fit linear regression models.

Stata Clinical Trial Data Hypothesis Testing Linear Regression

C-Reactive Protein and Anxiety Among Women in Houston

For my Field Epidemiology course, I worked with two classmates to develop a Manual of Procedures for a proposed study evaluating the association between C-reactive protein levels and anxiety among adult women residing in Houston.

The project required us to build each section of the study plan over the course of the semester, including procedures related to recruitment, field data collection, biospecimen handling, quality control, staff responsibilities, and standardized documentation.

Co-occurrence of Mental Health and Chronic Physical Conditions Among Asian Americans

For my Epidemiologic Methods in Population Health Differences course, I developed a presentation focused on the co-occurrence of mental health and chronic physical conditions among Asian Americans.

Using a social determinants of health approach, I described the population and health disparity, reviewed factors that may contribute to the disparity, identified gaps in the literature, and developed recommendations for future research.
