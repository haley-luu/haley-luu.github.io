Background and Interests

About Me

My background combines public health, research, healthcare, and data analysis.

My Background

I am currently completing an MPH in Epidemiology and a Data Science Certificate at UTHealth Houston School of Public Health. Before beginning my MPH, I completed an MS in Biology with a concentration in Translational and Clinical Research and a BS in Chemistry with a minor in Biology.

My professional background includes biomedical research, clinical laboratory work, pharmacy operations, public health informatics, healthcare data, and applied public health research. These experiences have helped me understand both the technical side of working with data and the importance of presenting information in a way that is accurate, organized, and useful.

What Interests Me

I am especially interested in public health data analysis, epidemiology, health informatics, mental health, chronic disease, and health disparities. I am drawn to work that helps explain why health outcomes differ across populations and how data can support better programs, policies, and healthcare decisions.

I also enjoy research and communication work that involves reviewing complex information, identifying meaningful patterns, and turning findings into clear summaries, reports, presentations, or recommendations.

How I Approach My Work

I tend to be careful and detail-oriented, especially when reviewing data, documentation, or research findings. I value accuracy, clear organization, and making sure that the reasoning behind an analysis or recommendation is easy to follow.

I am continuing to build my technical skills in R, Stata, SQL, and data visualization. My goal is not simply to write more complicated code, but to use the right tools to answer meaningful public health questions clearly and responsibly.
